History

Hyundai, originally founded as Hyundai Engineering and Construction Co. in 1947 by Chung Ju-yung, began as a construction firm. It rapidly expanded into various industries, including automotive and shipbuilding, eventually becoming South Korea's second-largest chaebol (conglomerate). Hyundai Motor Company, established in 1967, became the largest auto producer in South Korea.

​The 2025 Hyundai Santa Fe emerges as a standout in the midsize SUV segment, blending bold design, advanced technology, and family-friendly features. Its boxy, angular exterior is complemented by a spacious interior that accommodates up to seven passengers, offering ample legroom and a maximum cargo capacity of 79.6 cubic feet when the second and third rows are folded down. Under the hood, the Santa Fe offers two powertrain options: a robust 2.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivering 277 horsepower and 311 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed wet dual-clutch transmission ​; and a hybrid variant combining a 1.6-liter turbocharged engine with an electric motor, producing a combined 231 horsepower and achieving an estimated 36 mpg in the city .​ ​The 2025 Hyundai Ioniq 6 is a sleek, all-electric sedan that combines cutting-edge technology with impressive efficiency. Built on Hyundai's Electric Global Modular Platform E-GMP, it offers a range of powertrains to suit various driving preferences. The base model features a single electric motor producing 149 horsepower, while the Long-Range version boosts output to 225 horsepower. For those seeking enhanced performance, the dual-motor all-wheel-drive variant delivers a robust 320 horsepower, enabling acceleration from 0 to 60 mph in approximately 5.1 seconds. One of the Ioniq 6's standout features is its impressive driving range. Equipped with the larger 77.4 kWh battery, the rear-wheel-drive model can travel up to 361 miles on a single charge, according to EPA estimates. The vehicle supports 800V ultra-fast charging, allowing the battery to charge from 10% to 80% in just 18 minutes when using a 350 kW DC fast charger. ​The 2025 Hyundai Kona is a dynamic and versatile compact SUV that seamlessly blends modern design, advanced technology, and efficient performance. Its bold, futuristic exterior is complemented by a spacious interior, thanks to an extended wheelbase that enhances passenger comfort and cargo capacity. The cabin features a dual 12.3-inch panoramic display, integrating both the digital instrument cluster and the infotainment system, providing a high-tech and user-friendly interface. Connectivity is further enhanced with standard wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, along with Hyundai's Bluelink+ app compatibility for remote vehicle management. Under the hood, the 2025 Kona offers multiple powertrain options to suit diverse driving preferences. The base model is equipped with a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine producing 147 horsepower, while higher trims feature a 1.6-liter turbocharged engine delivering 190 horsepower. For eco-conscious drivers, the Kona Electric variant boasts a 201-horsepower motor with an estimated range of up to 261 miles on a single charge.
