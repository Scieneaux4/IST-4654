History

BMW's history began in 1916 as Bayerische Flugzeug-Werke Bavarian Aircraft Works, focusing on aircraft engines. After World War I, they transitioned to motorcycle production in 1923 and then entered the automobile business in 1928. Today, BMW is a major global manufacturer of premium automobiles and motorcycles.

The BMW M4 is a high-performance sports coupe that masterfully blends power, luxury, and advanced technology. At its core is a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged inline-six engine, delivering up to 543 horsepower in the track-focused M4 CS variant. The base M4 produces 473 hp with a six-speed manual and rear-wheel drive, while the M4 Competition boosts output to 503 hp with an eight-speed automatic and optional xDrive all-wheel drive. The M4 is engineered for agility, featuring Adaptive M Suspension, a carbon-fiber roof for weight reduction, and precise chassis tuning for superior handling. Inside, it boasts BMW’s iDrive 8.5 system with a curved digital display, wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, and optional carbon-fiber bucket seats. Design updates on the 2025 model include new laser-accented taillights, fresh alloy wheels, and aggressive aerodynamics. Whether in standard, Competition, or CS trim, the M4 offers a thrilling yet refined driving experience that embodies BMW’s motorsport heritage and innovation. The BMW X8 is an upcoming flagship luxury SUV that promises to combine high performance with top-tier comfort and innovation. Expected to launch in late 2025 or early 2026, the X8 will sit above the X7 in BMW’s lineup, featuring a bold, coupe-inspired design that emphasizes both sportiness and elegance. It is likely to be powered by a 4.4-liter twin-turbo V8 engine, delivering over 600 horsepower, with a plug-in hybrid variant also expected to enhance performance and efficiency. Inside, the X8 is designed to offer ultra-luxurious comfort, possibly including airline-style reclining rear seats, premium materials throughout the cabin, and a cutting-edge dual-screen infotainment system. BMW’s xDrive all-wheel-drive system, adaptive air suspension, and advanced driver assistance technologies will ensure a smooth and safe driving experience. Competing with high-end SUVs like the Mercedes-Maybach GLS and Range Rover, the X8 aims to set new benchmarks in the luxury SUV segment with its blend of dynamic power, refined design, and state-of-the-art features. The 2025 BMW M5 represents a bold new chapter in the M series, introducing a powerful plug-in hybrid system that merges exhilarating performance with modern efficiency. At its core is a 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine paired with a 194-hp electric motor and a 18.6 kWh battery, generating a combined output of 717 horsepower and 738 lb-ft of torque. This setup allows the M5 to sprint from 0 to 60 mph in just 3.4 seconds, while also offering an all-electric driving range of up to 67 kilometers. Despite weighing over 5,300 pounds, the M5 maintains sharp handling thanks to its M xDrive all-wheel-drive system with a rear-wheel-drive mode, adaptive M suspension, and rear-axle steering. Inside, the cabin is outfitted with BMW’s latest curved iDrive display, luxurious materials, and a premium Bowers & Wilkins sound system.
