History

Mercedes-Benz's history begins with Karl Benz and Gottlieb Daimler independently inventing the automobile in the late 19th century. The name "Mercedes" originated from Emil Jellinek, an Austrian entrepreneur, who named a high-performance Daimler model after his daughter, Mercedes. In 1926, Benz and Daimler's companies merged, forming Daimler-Benz AG, and the Mercedes-Benz brand was established.

The Mercedes-Benz AMG G63 is a luxurious yet fiercely capable SUV that blends high-performance engineering with iconic design. Under the hood, it houses a powerful 4.0-liter V8 biturbo engine producing 577 horsepower and 627 lb-ft of torque, allowing it to accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in around 4.5 seconds. Paired with a 9-speed AMG SPEEDSHIFT® TCT transmission and 4MATIC all-wheel drive, the G63 delivers both smooth on-road dynamics and rugged off-road prowess. It features three locking differentials, low-range gearing, and an advanced off-road display system, making it one of the few luxury SUVs that can truly handle extreme terrain. The Mercedes-Benz AMG G-Wagon, officially known as the Mercedes-AMG G63, is a high-performance luxury SUV that blends iconic off-road heritage with modern-day power and technology. It features a handcrafted 4.0-liter V8 biturbo engine that delivers an impressive 577 horsepower and 627 lb-ft of torque, allowing it to accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just about 4.5 seconds. Paired with the AMG SPEEDSHIFT® TCT 9-speed transmission and a performance-tuned 4MATIC+ all-wheel drive system, the G63 offers exhilarating speed with full off-road capability, supported by three locking differentials and a low-range gearbox. The Mercedes-Benz AMG GT is a stunning blend of motorsport heritage and luxury craftsmanship, designed to deliver an exhilarating driving experience. At its heart lies a handcrafted 4.0-liter V8 biturbo engine that produces up to 577 horsepower depending on the trim, with rear-wheel drive and a lightning-quick AMG SPEEDSHIFT® DCT 7-speed dual-clutch transmission. This powerhouse setup allows the AMG GT to accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in as little as 3.5 seconds, making it one of the most thrilling road-legal sports cars in the Mercedes lineup. The AMG Ride Control suspension, active aerodynamics, and limited-slip rear differential ensure razor-sharp handling and cornering, whether on the highway or the racetrack.
