History

Ferrari's history begins with Enzo Ferrari's passion for racing, leading him to found Scuderia Ferrari in 1929 and later, Ferrari S.p.A. in 1947. The company initially focused on high-performance sports cars and quickly gained recognition through racing victories. Ferrari's success in Formula One and other racing events has cemented its legacy as a leader in the automotive industry.

The Ferrari SF90 Stradale is a groundbreaking plug-in hybrid supercar that combines cutting-edge performance with innovative technology. It features a 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine paired with three electric motors, delivering a combined output of 986 horsepower and enabling the car to accelerate from 0 to 100 km/h in just 2.5 seconds. This is the first Ferrari mid-engine model to feature all-wheel drive, providing enhanced traction and stability. The SF90 offers four driving modes through its advanced eManettino selector: eDrive for all-electric cruising, Hybrid for efficiency, Performance for spirited driving, and Qualify for maximum output. It also incorporates advanced systems like Side Slip Control and torque vectoring for superior handling. The car’s chassis is constructed from lightweight materials like carbon fiber and aluminum, complemented by active aerodynamics that adapt to driving conditions. Inside, the SF90 blends luxury and technology with a 16-inch curved digital display, touch-sensitive controls on the steering wheel, and premium materials such as Alcantara and carbon fiber. The Ferrari F8 Tributo is a stunning mid-engine supercar that celebrates the legacy of Ferrari’s V8 performance lineage with a perfect blend of power, precision, and style. At its heart is a 3.9-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine that produces an impressive 710 horsepower, enabling the F8 to sprint from 0 to 100 km/h in just 2.9 seconds and reach a top speed of 340 km/h. The F8 is built on a lightweight architecture, combining carbon fiber and aluminum to enhance agility and responsiveness. Advanced aerodynamics, inspired by Ferrari's track experience, include features like the S-Duct and active rear spoilers, which boost downforce and stability at high speeds. The car is equipped with Ferrari’s latest vehicle dynamics systems such as Side Slip Angle Control and the Ferrari Dynamic Enhancer, offering drivers exceptional handling and control, even at the limit. Inside, the F8 features a driver-focused cockpit with a mix of luxury and high-tech elements, including a digital instrument cluster, tactile controls on the steering wheel, and optional carbon fiber trim. ​The Ferrari Roma is a front mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive 2+2 grand tourer that blends classic Italian elegance with modern performance and technology. Powered by a 3.9-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine, it delivers 612 horsepower and 561 lb-ft of torque, enabling acceleration from 0 to 100 km/h in just 3.4 seconds and reaching a top speed of 199 mph. The engine is paired with an 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission, contributing to the car's dynamic driving experience.​ The Roma's design pays homage to Ferrari's grand touring heritage, featuring clean lines, flush door handles, and slim LED lights. An active rear spoiler adjusts to driving conditions, enhancing aerodynamics and stability at high speeds. Inside, the cabin offers a driver-centric layout with a 16-inch digital instrument cluster and an 8.4-inch central touchscreen. Optional features include an 8.8-inch passenger-side display, allowing co-drivers to interact with navigation and media controls.
